I am a young Engineer aspiring to work at the intersection of Data Engineering and AI/ML Development

When I was young, I was a massive geography nerd and was fascinated with Urban Planning and Architecture. Although coding Minecraft plugins in Java at 12 years old steered me into the tech space, my main project at the time was building cities in Minecraft. This unlocked many opportunities for me, such as holding presentations and workshops at events across the country and developing a game for the M9 Museum in Italy. These experiences ultimately led me to attend Cornell. Early on, I combined my tech interests with my passion for Urban Planning and Architecture by taking big data courses offered by the City and Regional Planning major. I declared as an Operations Research & Engineering major, as I felt this best fit this niche combo of interests. However, after taking Machine Learning coursework, I found a similar fascination and elegance in Machine Learning algorithms and Deep Networks as I did in the layout of cities and design of buildings. This led to me deeper into ML via coursework in Deep Learning, Robot/Reinforcement Learning, and even Honors Real Analysis (MATH 4130). I found an even deeper interest in various optimization algorithms, leading me to take on an independent research project in Simulation Optimization. My practical development along with these interests included a Data Analytics and Research internship at Paramount and a Data Engineering internship at Mastercard. Ultimately, my practical work with data engineering and academic work in the ML space has led me to seek roles at the intersection of these two worlds.
